# Research Status

Date: 2026-06-28

This project is now a safety-oriented C-MAPSS RUL evaluation benchmark covering
FD001-FD004. It should be framed as evaluation protocol plus risk-trade-off
evidence, not as a new state-of-the-art architecture paper or an aviation safety
certification claim.

## Confirmed Paper Direction

Working title:

> Safety-Oriented Evaluation of Classical and Deep Sequence Models for
> Aero-Engine RUL Prediction on C-MAPSS

Central research question:

> On FD001-FD004, is the aggregate RMSE-best model also the best model for
> late-life error and optimistic overestimation risk?

Current answer: no. RMSE-best, critical-zone-best, overestimation-risk-best, and
SARBI-best rankings often differ.

## Completed Evidence

- Representative FD001-FD004 model matrix for Ridge, Random Forest, Gradient
  Boosting, GRU, and Safety-GRU.
- Full GRU safety-loss ablation: 4 subsets x 3 seeds x 8 jobs = 96 jobs.
- Leakage-aware protocol: engine-level validation split, train-only scaling,
  capped RUL labels, window size 30, last-window test evaluation, seeds 42/43/44.
- Metrics: RMSE, MAE, NASA S-score, Critical RMSE30/50, overestimation ratio,
  and overestimation magnitude.
- SARBI reporting layer: a relative composite score that combines aggregate
  accuracy, late-life error, and optimistic-risk behavior while keeping raw
  metrics visible.
- Bootstrap checks for RMSE-best versus SARBI-best comparisons on the
  representative matrix.

## Key Findings

- FD001: Gradient Boosting remains RMSE/SARBI-best in the representative matrix,
  but Safety-GRU wins critical RMSE and overestimation-risk metrics. In the GRU
  ablation, safety-w3 is SARBI-best and wins multiple risk metrics.
- FD002: GRU is RMSE-best in the representative matrix, while Safety-GRU is
  SARBI/risk-best. In the ablation, critical/asymmetric/safety losses split the
  risk-metric wins.
- FD003: Results are mixed. RMSE and NASA can align for the ablation, but
  critical and overestimation-risk winners still differ.
- FD004: strongest evidence for the thesis. Random Forest is RMSE-best in the
  representative matrix and baseline GRU is RMSE-best in the ablation, while
  Safety-GRU or safety losses win SARBI, critical-zone, and overestimation-risk
  metrics.

## Manuscript Status

`reports/paper/main.tex` has been rewritten from the old FD001/FD003 framing to
the FD001-FD004 safety-oriented evaluation paper. The Results section is now
organized around ranking reversals and accuracy-risk trade-offs rather than a
single best model claim.

## Deferred Work

Uncertainty, maintenance-decision simulation, domain shift, robustness, and XAI
remain future-work scaffolds. They should not be promoted to the first paper's
main contribution until the safety-oriented benchmark paper is stable.

## Scope Boundaries

- C-MAPSS is simulated benchmark data, not real fleet telemetry.
- Safety-GRU means loss weighting, not a certified safety method.
- SARBI is a reporting index, not a physical RUL predictor or certification
  formula.
- Three seeds support benchmark evidence but should be discussed with cautious
  uncertainty language.
